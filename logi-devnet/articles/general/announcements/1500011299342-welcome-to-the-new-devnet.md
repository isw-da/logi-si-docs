# Welcome to the New DevNet 

Hello!

Logi Analytics is proud and excited to announce the relaunch of our Logi DevNet Community. Our core focus for this upgrade has been around experience and content. To fully benefit from the changes we have made, make sure you sign in to your DevNet profile, or create a new one if you don’t have one (login information is in the top right corner!).

**Content**

First, we wanted to ensure that all content relevant to Logi’s solutions are now centralized in one space. For any new Logi users – or those looking for a refresh – check out the Getting Started guides located on each product page ([Composer](https://devnet.logianalytics.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500010961142-Get-Started-with-Composer-v6), [Report](https://devnet.logianalytics.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500009854341-Getting-Started-with-Logi-Report-Designer-v18), [Info](https://devnet.logianalytics.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500009533842-Logi-Info-v12-8-Overview)). Another key upgrade is the ability to comment and rate the documentation. This way you can quickly let us know if there are any issues or concerns with an article or document and also rate it to let us know its usefulness, which will help your peers quickly discover highly rated content.

**Experience**

You may have noticed the fresh look and feel of DevNet. In addition, we have reorganized the structure to make it more product centric. You can now navigate to each product using the product cards or the top navigation menu bar. Within each product page you’ll find documentation, knowledge base, forums, and the “getting started” guide for that product.

Another upgrade is our enhanced search.  From the main page, you now have a federated Search experience of all content within DevNet (including the documentation). When you are in an individual product section, such as Logi Composer, your search results would feature content specifically for that product.

**Forums**

The forums are the place to go if you have any questions about our products, wish to discuss a use case or implementation with a fellow Logi user, or just explore what other Logi enthusiasts are saying about the latest releases. Please remember to sign in (or sign up) to get the full benefits of the forums.

So please, start exploring, share with your teammates, and start connecting with other community users!

The DevNet Team
